## 1. Income Tax Bands and Rates

### 2024/25 Tax Year (6 April 2024 -- 5 April 2025)

| Band | Taxable Income | Rate |
|------|---------------|------|
| Personal Allowance | Up to GBP 12,570 | 0% |
| Basic Rate | GBP 12,571 -- GBP 50,270 | 20% |
| Higher Rate | GBP 50,271 -- GBP 125,140 | 40% |
| Additional Rate | Over GBP 125,140 | 45% |

**Key notes:**
- Personal Allowance is reduced by GBP 1 for every GBP 2 of income above GBP 100,000
- Personal Allowance is zero when income exceeds GBP 125,140
- The Personal Allowance has been frozen at GBP 12,570 until April 2028
- Marriage Allowance: transfer up to GBP 1,260 to a spouse (worth up to GBP 252)
- Blind Person's Allowance: GBP 2,870

### 2025/26 Tax Year (6 April 2025 -- 5 April 2026)

| Higher Rate | GBP 50,271 -- GBP 125,140 | 40% |
| Additional Rate | Over GBP 125,140 | 45% |

**Note:** The thresholds remain frozen as part of the government's fiscal drag policy through to April 2028.

## 2. Corporation Tax

### Rates (from 1 April 2023, continuing into 2025/26)

| Profits | Rate |
|---------|------|
| Up to GBP 50,000 (Small Profits Rate) | 19% |
| GBP 50,001 -- GBP 250,000 (Marginal Relief) | 19-25% (effective ~26.5% marginal) |
| Over GBP 250,000 (Main Rate) | 25% |

**Key notes:**
- Associated companies share the thresholds (divide by number of associated companies)
- Marginal Relief formula: (GBP 250,000 - Profits) x (Taxable Profits / Profits) x 3/200
- Corporation tax is due 9 months and 1 day after the end of the accounting period
- CT600 return due 12 months after end of accounting period
- Large companies (profits over GBP 1.5 million) pay in quarterly instalments

## 3. VAT (Value Added Tax)

### Registration Thresholds

| Period | Threshold |
|--------|-----------|
| 2024/25 | GBP 90,000 (from 1 April 2024) |
| 2025/26 | GBP 90,000 (unchanged) |

**Deregistration threshold:** GBP 88,000

### VAT Rates

| Type | Rate | Examples |
|------|------|----------|
| Standard | 20% | Most goods and services |
| Reduced | 5% | Home energy, children's car seats, sanitary products |
| Zero-rated | 0% | Most food, children's clothing, books, newspapers |
| Exempt | N/A | Insurance, education, health services, land |

### VAT Schemes

**Flat Rate Scheme:**
- For businesses with VAT-taxable turnover under GBP 150,000
- Pay a fixed percentage of gross turnover
- First-year discount: 1% off the flat rate
- Rates vary by industry (e.g., IT consultancy 14.5%, general retail 4%)

**Cash Accounting Scheme:**
- For businesses with taxable turnover under GBP 1,350,000
- Account for VAT when payment is received/made, not when invoiced

**Annual Accounting Scheme:**
- For businesses with taxable turnover under GBP 1,350,000
- One annual VAT return instead of quarterly
- Pay in monthly or quarterly instalments

**Key dates:**
- VAT returns: Quarterly, due 1 month and 7 days after the end of the period
- Digital submission required under Making Tax Digital

## 4. Self Assessment

### Key Deadlines

| Deadline | Date |
|----------|------|
| Tax year ends | 5 April |
| Register for Self Assessment (new businesses) | 5 October following the tax year |
| Paper return deadline | 31 October following the tax year |
| Online return deadline | 31 January following the tax year |
| Payment of tax owed | 31 January following the tax year |
| First payment on account | 31 January |
| Second payment on account | 31 July |
| Balancing payment | 31 January (following year) |

### Who Must File

- Sole traders with income over GBP 1,000
- Partners in a business partnership
- Company directors (in some cases)
- Individuals with income from property over GBP 1,000
- Anyone with untaxed income over GBP 2,500
- Higher-rate taxpayers with savings or investment income
- Anyone with capital gains to report
- Non-resident landlords

### Payments on Account

- Each payment on account is 50% of the previous year's tax bill
- Not required if last year's tax bill was under GBP 1,000
- Not required if more than 80% of tax was collected at source
- Can apply to reduce payments on account if income is expected to fall

### Penalties

| Offence | Penalty |
|---------|---------|
| 1 day late | GBP 100 |
| 3 months late | GBP 10 per day (up to 90 days = GBP 900) |
| 6 months late | GBP 300 or 5% of tax due (whichever is greater) |
| 12 months late | GBP 300 or 5% of tax due (serious cases up to 100%) |
| Late payment (30 days) | 5% of tax outstanding |
| Late payment (6 months) | Additional 5% |
| Late payment (12 months) | Additional 5% |

## 5. Capital Gains Tax (CGT)

### Annual Exempt Amount

| Tax Year | Allowance |
|----------|-----------|
| 2024/25 | GBP 3,000 |
| 2025/26 | GBP 3,000 |

### Rates

| Taxpayer | Residential Property | Other Assets |
|----------|---------------------|-------------|
| Basic rate | 18% | 10% |
| Higher rate | 24% | 20% |

**Note:** From 30 October 2024, the lower CGT rate on other assets increased from 10% to 18% for basic rate, and from 20% to 24% for higher rate. Residential property rates remain at 18% and 24%.

### Key Reliefs

- **Business Asset Disposal Relief (formerly Entrepreneurs' Relief):** 10% rate on qualifying gains up to GBP 1 million lifetime limit
- **Investors' Relief:** 10% rate on qualifying shares up to GBP 10 million
- **Private Residence Relief:** Main home exempt from CGT
- **Letting Relief:** Up to GBP 40,000 if property was main home and let out
- **Annual Exempt Amount:** Use it or lose it each year
- **Losses:** Can be offset against gains in the same or future years
- **Spouse transfers:** No CGT on transfers between spouses/civil partners

### Reporting

- Residential property disposals must be reported within 60 days of completion
- Non-property gains reported via Self Assessment

## 7. National Insurance Contributions (NICs)

### 2025/26 Rates

#### Class 1 (Employees)

| Earnings | Rate |
|----------|------|
| Up to GBP 12,570 (Primary Threshold) | 0% |
| GBP 12,570 -- GBP 50,270 (Upper Earnings Limit) | 8% |
| Above GBP 50,270 | 2% |

#### Class 1 (Employers)

| Earnings | Rate |
|----------|------|
| Up to GBP 5,000 (Secondary Threshold, from April 2025) | 0% |
| Above GBP 5,000 | 15% |

**Note:** From April 2025, employer NIC rate increased from 13.8% to 15%, and the secondary threshold reduced from GBP 9,100 to GBP 5,000.

#### Class 2 (Self-Employed)

- Abolished from April 2024
- Voluntary contributions available at GBP 3.45 per week for NI record gaps

#### Class 4 (Self-Employed)

| Profits | Rate |
|---------|------|
| Up to GBP 12,570 (Lower Profits Limit) | 0% |
| GBP 12,570 -- GBP 50,270 (Upper Profits Limit) | 6% |
| Above GBP 50,270 | 2% |

### Employment Allowance

- GBP 10,500 per year (from April 2025, increased from GBP 5,000)
- Offsets employer NIC liability
- Available to all eligible employers (the GBP 100,000 threshold was removed from April 2025)
- Not available to single-director companies with no other employees

## 8. Pension Contributions

### Annual Allowance

| Tax Year | Standard | Money Purchase (if triggered) |
|----------|----------|------------------------------|
| 2024/25 | GBP 60,000 | GBP 10,000 |
| 2025/26 | GBP 60,000 | GBP 10,000 |

### Key Rules

- **Tax relief:** Contributions receive tax relief at the individual's marginal rate
- **Carry forward:** Unused allowance from the previous 3 years can be carried forward
- **Tapered Annual Allowance:** Reduced for those with adjusted income over GBP 260,000 (minimum GBP 10,000)
- **Lifetime Allowance:** Abolished from April 2024
- **Employer contributions:** Deductible as a business expense, no NIC due
- **Salary sacrifice:** Efficient for both employer and employee (saves employer NIC)

### Tax Relief Methods

- **Relief at source:** Pension provider claims basic rate (20%) automatically; higher/additional rate claimed via Self Assessment
- **Net pay arrangement:** Full relief given through payroll

## 10. R&D Tax Credits

### SME Scheme (for accounting periods beginning before 1 April 2024)

- Enhanced deduction of 86% on qualifying R&D expenditure (reduced from 130%)
- Tax credit of 10% for loss-making SMEs (reduced from 14.5%)
- "R&D intensive" SMEs (40%+ of total expenditure on R&D) get 14.5% credit rate

### Merged Scheme (from 1 April 2024)

- Single scheme replacing SME and RDEC schemes
- 20% above-the-line credit on qualifying expenditure
- Effective benefit: 15% after corporation tax at 25%
- R&D intensive companies (30%+ of total expenditure): Enhanced rate of 27% credit for loss-makers

### What Qualifies

- Projects that seek to achieve an advance in science or technology
- Must involve resolving scientific or technological uncertainty
- The advance must not be readily deducible by a competent professional

### Qualifying Expenditure

- Staff costs (salaries, NIC, pension contributions)
- Subcontractor costs (65% for connected parties, relevant percentage for unconnected)
- Software and consumables used in R&D
- Cloud computing and data costs (from April 2023)

### Claims Process

- Claimed through the CT600 corporation tax return
- Additional information form required from 1 August 2023
- Must notify HMRC of intention to claim within 6 months of period end (for first-time or lapsed claimants)

## 12. IR35 (Off-Payroll Working)

### Overview

IR35 determines whether a contractor is genuinely self-employed or is effectively an employee for tax purposes.

### Responsibility for Determination

| Client Type | Who Decides |
|-------------|-------------|
| Private sector (medium/large) | The client (end hirer) |
| Private sector (small) | The contractor (PSC) |
| Public sector | The client (end hirer) |

### "Small Company" Exemption (private sector)

A company is "small" if it meets two or more of:
- Annual turnover not more than GBP 10.2 million
- Balance sheet total not more than GBP 5.1 million
- Not more than 50 employees

### Key Tests for Employment Status

| Factor | Employee-like | Self-employed-like |
|--------|--------------|-------------------|
| Control | Client dictates how, when, where | Contractor decides working methods |
| Substitution | Must do the work personally | Can send a substitute |
| Mutuality of obligation | Ongoing obligation to provide/accept work | Project-by-project basis |
| Financial risk | No financial risk | Bears own costs, risk of loss |
| Equipment | Provided by client | Uses own equipment |
| Part of organisation | Integrated into client's team | Independent business |
| Exclusivity | Works for one client | Multiple clients |

### Consequences of Being Inside IR35

- Tax and NIC deducted as if employed
- The fee-payer (agency or client) is responsible for deductions
- 5% allowance for expenses removed (from April 2021 for medium/large private sector)

### HMRC Tool

- **CEST (Check Employment Status for Tax):** HMRC's online tool for determining IR35 status
- Available at gov.uk -- not always definitive but provides a defensible position

## 13. Business Structure Comparisons

### Sole Trader vs Limited Company vs LLP

| Factor | Sole Trader | Limited Company | LLP |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|-----|
| Setup cost | Free (just register for SA) | GBP 12-50 (Companies House) | GBP 12-50 |
| Legal status | Not separate from owner | Separate legal entity | Separate legal entity |
| Liability | Unlimited personal liability | Limited to company assets | Limited to contribution |
| Tax on profits | Income Tax + Class 4 NIC | Corporation Tax | Income Tax (each partner) |
| Taking money out | All profit is yours | Salary + dividends | Profit share drawings |
| Accounting | Simple records, SA return | Annual accounts + CT600 + SA | Annual accounts + SA per partner |
| Privacy | Name and address on HMRC only | Directors/shareholders public | Members public |
| National Insurance | Class 4 NIC on profits | Employer + Employee NIC on salary | Class 4 NIC on profit share |
| Admin burden | Low | Medium-High | Medium |
| Raising finance | Harder | Easier (share issuance) | Moderate |

### When to Consider Incorporating (Sole Trader to Limited Company)

A limited company may be more tax-efficient when:
- Profits consistently exceed GBP 30,000-50,000 per year
- You do not need all the profit for personal living expenses
- You want to retain profits in the business
- You need liability protection
- You are taking on employees or significant contracts

### Tax Comparison Example (2025/26, single person, no other income)

**Scenario: GBP 60,000 profit**

**As Sole Trader:**
- Income Tax: GBP 11,432
- Class 4 NIC: GBP 2,254 + Class 2: GBP 0 (abolished)
- Total tax: approximately GBP 13,686
- Take-home: approximately GBP 46,314

**As Limited Company (optimal salary GBP 12,570 + dividends):**
- Corporation Tax (25% on GBP 47,430): GBP 9,486 (small profits rate 19% if no associated companies: GBP 9,012)
- Employer NIC on salary: approximately GBP 1,137 (above GBP 5,000 threshold at 15%)
- Income Tax on dividends: approximately GBP 2,654
- Employee NIC: GBP 0 (salary at Personal Allowance)
- Total tax: approximately GBP 12,803
- Saving vs sole trader: approximately GBP 883

**Note:** The benefit increases with higher profits and if profits can be retained in the company.

## 14. Property Income

### Allowable Expenses for Landlords

- Mortgage interest: Tax credit at 20% (not a deduction from profit)
- Insurance (buildings and contents)
- Letting agent fees and management costs
- Maintenance and repairs (not improvements)
- Council tax and utility bills (if paid by landlord)
- Ground rent and service charges
- Accountancy fees
- Legal fees for lets of a year or less
- Cost of replacing furnishings (Replacement of Domestic Items Relief)

### Rent-a-Room Relief

- Tax-free income of GBP 7,500 per year
- For letting furnished accommodation in your main home
- Automatically applied if income is under the threshold
- Can elect to use the "alternative method" (actual profit/loss) if expenses are high

### Property Allowance

- GBP 1,000 tax-free property income per year
- Cannot be combined with claiming actual expenses

### Furnished Holiday Lettings (FHL)

- The FHL tax regime is being abolished from April 2025
- Previously offered favourable tax treatment including capital allowances on furnishings, CGT reliefs, and pension relevant earnings
